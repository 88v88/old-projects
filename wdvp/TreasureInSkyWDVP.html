"Outer space, including the moon and other celestial bodies, is not subject to national appropriation by claim of sovereignty, by means of use or occupation, or by any other means."

Outer Space Treaty, Article II

Before asteroid mining can be regulated, taxed, or whatsover, it has to be legalized. The above article from the Outer Space Treaty, written over fifty years ago, is open to interpretation on whether asteroid mining is allowed.

The treaty should be revised to clarify these new situations. Afterwards, governments come into play. They are responsible for monitoring space mining activity and creating paperwork to ensure sensible operations.

So how can we improve the technology?

areas of improvement

Alternative Propulsion

Propellant-less systems like solar and electric sails are being researched as alternative propulsion methods for spaceships, and they perform best over long distance.

Solar sails can be used to reach distant asteroids without spending much fuel.

Fuel Conversion

The most common type of asteroid contains water ice and organic substances which can be converted into fuel so less fuel would have to be launched.

The asteroid mining company Planetary Resources plans to mine water for fuel to increase the spaceship's efficiency when later moving on to mining metal.

Atmospheric Entry

The Problem
1. Object returns to Earth's atmosphere
2. Its speed frictionally heats and ionizes air.
3. Nitrogen oxides are more powerful greenhouse gases than CO2 created

New ways to enter the atmosphere should be researched to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and prevent degradation of mined material.
